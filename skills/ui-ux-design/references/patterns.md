# Common Layout Patterns

## Dashboards
- Key metrics ("KPIs") at the top as scannable cards, most important leftmost.
- Group related charts/tables under clear section headings.
- Use consistent chart color coding across the whole dashboard (a metric is always the same color).
- Provide filters/date range at a consistent, predictable location (usually top-right or a left rail).

## Forms
- Single column for most forms — multi-column forms hurt completion rates and scanning.
- Group related fields visually (e.g. "Shipping Address" as a block) with subtle section spacing, not just a divider line.
- Put the primary submit action bottom-right (LTR) or full-width on mobile; keep it visually dominant.
- Show progress for multi-step forms (numbered steps or a progress bar), not just "page 2 of 5" in tiny text.

## Landing pages
- Lead with a specific value proposition, not a vague tagline — say what the product does in the first sentence.
- Vary section rhythm (don't repeat the exact same "image left, text right" block 5 times).
- Social proof (logos, testimonials, numbers) placed near the decision point, not just at the bottom.

## Data tables
- Right-align numeric columns, left-align text columns.
- Sticky header row for long tables.
- Zebra striping or subtle row dividers, not both.
- Sort/filter affordances visible, not hidden behind a hover-only interaction.
